===== slide10.xml =====
The difference between Essential features, requirements and success criteria
Essential features
A list of all the features your software must do in order to do its job
This could be identified previously from evaluating similar systems
Requirements
These are things the system requires
It could be data or a dataset
It will be software including libraries
It will be hardware: Provide a minimum PC spec for the program
It is understandable and common to confuse them! Here are the differences.

Describing computational methods you will use to solve the problem
Large complex problems are solved by the following computational methods
Do NOT be too verbose on this!
Stick to brief descriptions of how you will use each method for your project!
Thinking Abstractly &amp; Visualisation 
Removing unnecessary detail 
Thinking Ahead  
Identify the inputs and outputs for a given situation. Determine the preconditions for devising a solution to a problem. The need for reusable program components
Thinking Procedurally &amp; Decomposition
(a) Identify the components of a problem. (b) Identify the components of a solution to a problem. (c) Determine the order of the steps needed to solve a problem. (d) Identify sub-procedures necessary to solve a problem.
